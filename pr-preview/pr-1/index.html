Drag a tent onto the plan, or click it then click the plan. Each tent is placed once and leaves the list. Double-click a placed tent to edit it. Select a placed tent to rotate (left / right arrows, Shift = fine, or the rotate buttons); delete it to return it to the list.
Electrical view. Add a Source (a popup sets its amps and outputs) and cabinets from the toolbar. To wire, turn on the Wire tool, click a source/cabinet, then click what it feeds. With Wire off, clicking just selects and highlights the supply chain. Cabinets are three-phase: single-phase 220V loads are balanced across L1/L2/L3, three-phase loads sit on all phases, and any phase over the rating is flagged red. Click a cable to remove it; select a node and press Delete.
Water view. Add a Brunn (water source) and Handfat (sink) from the toolbar, then turn on the Wire tool and click a brunn followed by a handfat or water object to run a blue pipe. Click a pipe to remove it; select a fixture and press Delete.

Power source

Set the rating and which outputs this unit provides. The rating applies per phase (L1/L2/L3), so a 63A unit carries 63A on each of L1/L2/L3 (three 32A tents fit easily, one per phase).

Set the scale

Find something in your image whose real length you know — a path length, a building edge, the width of the open field. Click Start, then click its two ends on the image. You'll then enter that real length in metres.
